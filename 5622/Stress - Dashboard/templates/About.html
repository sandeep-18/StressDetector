Stress Detector

Why stress?

Stress can be defined as a physical, emotional or mental factor that can cause mental or bodily tension in the body. The World Health Organization (WHO) has mentioned that stress has been categorized as the health epidemic of the 21st century which means it is becoming a part of people's normal life. In addition, according to Australian Psychological Society (APS), 35% of Australian have reported experiencing significant level of distress while 26% experiencing anxiety in the moderate to extremely level. Stress can be helpful in the short time as it may motivate people to do things done , however in the long term it can affect their health. When a person’s body is under stress, chemical changes will occur in the body in response to stress. As an example, adrenaline and cortisol hormones rush may occur which can have side effects on the body such as high blood pressure, decreasing digestive system activity, converting glycogen to glucose by the liver, etc.

Used sensors:



Heart rate (pulse oximeter)

A pulse oximeter is a small, lightweight device used to monitor the amount of oxygen carried in the body. This noninvasive tool attaches painlessly to your fingertip, sending two wavelengths of light through the finger to measure your pulse rate and how much oxygen is in your system.

Body tempreture sesor

The temperature sensor allows you to measure this key parameter for body monitoring. Its measure the human body temperature with a 0.1 ºC maximum deviation. The sensor needs to be connected to the specific Temperature jack connector in MySignals board and it works with direct connector power supply. The sensor includes an armband in order to facilitate the laying of the sensor, keeping the hands free.
